My Reflection: Living in the IT Era

Living in the IT Era has completely transformed the way people communicate, access information, and build relationships. As an AB Communication student, I realized that technology is not just a tool for convenience but also a powerful force that shapes society.

Throughout this subject, I learned about cybersecurity, digital responsibility, online ethics, and the risks of misinformation. These topics opened my eyes to the reality that communication in the digital world carries serious responsibilities.

Technology allows information to spread instantly. Because of this, communication professionals must be careful, critical, and ethical in creating and sharing content. A single post can influence opinions, shape beliefs, and even affect communities.

I also learned that digital literacy is very important. It is not enough to simply use social media or technology. We must understand how platforms work, how data is collected, and how information can be manipulated.

This subject helped me become more aware of my digital behavior. I became more cautious about what I share online and more critical about the information I consume. It strengthened my sense of responsibility as a future professional.

In the future, I plan to use technology as a platform for positive communication. I want to promote truth, creativity, and ethical media practices. Living in the IT Era is both an opportunity and a responsibility, and I am ready to embrace both.
